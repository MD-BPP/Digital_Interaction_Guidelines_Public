Dr Sokratis Dinos, PhD

Director of Psychology programmes

Sokratis Dinos is a Chartered Psychologist, an Associate Fellow of the British Psychological Society and the head of psychology at BPP University. Sokratis has worked as a senior researcher and psychology lecturer at Queen Mary, University College London and University of Surrey in the UK. He has also worked as a consultant for the Department of Health in the UK shaping the government’s mental health strategy in the White Paper and has further worked as a research consultant for a number of independent organisations. Sokratis has led the national evaluation of a number of government initiatives in the UK regarding services for parents and young people as well as in delivering equality in health care.

Sokratis is in the editorial board of Psychiatric Bulletin and a reviewer for large programme grants for major UK funding bodies. He recently edited a book on ‘Becoming a Psychologist’ and his work has been published in a number of high impact journals including the British Journal of Psychiatry, the International Journal of Epidemiology and Social Science and Medicine.

Dr Dimitra Pachi, PhD, CPsychol

Senior Lecturer, Developmental Psychology

Dimitra is a Chartered Psychologist with the British Psychological society and a socio-political researcher with a strong interest in processes of civic/political participation during childhood and adolescence. She has worked for the University of Surrey as an associate Lecturer and a Research fellow for a number of years, and for third sector and private organisations as a consultant.

As a researcher, she has conducted multimethodological work both for EU funded international projects and national case study projects. She has published a number of chapters in highly acclaimed books in her field, such as The Palgrave Handbook of Global Political Psychology, and scientific journals.

Her expertise is on political/civic participation and inter/intra-national conflict resolution with a focus on social and political trust, political efficacy, group identification and inter-group emotions.

Dr. Petia Paramova-Castano, PhD, CPsychol

Senior Lecturer, Occupational Psychology

Petia obtained her Bachelor’s degree in Psychology, her Master’s in Occupational Psychology and her PhD from Goldsmiths College, University of London. Her expertise is in the area of political leadership with special interest in cross-cultural assessment and selection of politicians.

Prior to joining BPP Petia taught a wide range of psychology topics at Goldsmiths College, while at Newham College University Centre she helped develop the BPS-accredited BSc Psychology programme. Petia is an associate fellow of the Higher Education Academy

Dr David S. Smith, PhD, CPsychol, AFBPsS

Lecturer, Biological Psychology

David studied his undergraduate and doctorate at the University of Aberdeen, where his interdisciplinary research merged cognitive psychology with social perception research to study how sociosexual pressures may have shaped women’s episodic memory systems.

He has presented his research at international conferences, and his work has been published in peer-reviewed journals including Memory & Cognition and Evolution and Human Behavior.

Before finishing his doctorate David was a tutor at the University of Aberdeen, where he taught advanced methodology. More recently he has worked as a lecturer and course leader for a number of Open University validated programs at Newham University Centre.
