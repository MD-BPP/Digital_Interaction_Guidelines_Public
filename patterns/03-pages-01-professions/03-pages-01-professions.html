Your career in law

Whatever your level of study, we will develop you into commercially-focused, highly employable solicitors or barristers. Give your law career the best possible start by making us your first choice for legal education.

  • Solicitor
  • Barrister

A solicitor provides expert legal advice to clients by interpreting and applying the law to their situation in order to advise them on their best course of action.

Some of the most popular areas to practice in include private client, corporate, commercial, criminal, administratice and public law, charities, employment and family law. Working as a solicitor is undoubtedly a challenging and demanding career choice. You will need energy, vision and a sharp mind to complement your studies and develop successfully in the legal profession.

How we'll get you there

A barrister provides specialist legal advice, and represents individual people and organisations in courts and tribunals.

In general, Barristers are hired by solicitors to represent a case in court and only become involved once advocacy before a court is needed. The role of a Barrister is to advocate on behalf of their clients, presenting their case, examining and cross-examining witnesses and giving reasons why the court should support the case. They will then negotiate settlements with the other side.
